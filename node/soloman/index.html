Climate Justice & Survival Plan

Context • Frontline Island Vulnerability

Sea-level rise • Cyclones • Small-island economies • Food + energy security

Narrative: The youth of the Solomon Islands are tide-keepers at the edge of the world. Ancestral wayfinding meets a changed ocean. The task is to hold culture while adapting with precision: climate literacy, reef healing, storm-safe housing, and dignified livelihoods that do not require leaving home.

Scientific signal: Rising seas, stronger cyclones, marine heatwaves, and saltwater intrusion threaten agriculture, water, and coastal villages. Adaptive strategies include mangrove restoration, ridge-to-reef watershed management, cyclone-resilient building codes, decentralized renewable energy, and food sovereignty models suited to atolls and high islands.

  • Zero-harm • Youth safety first
  • Sovereignty • Community-led decisions

Education & Skills

Climate literacy • Ocean stewardship • Renewable energy • Blue/green jobs

Curriculum tracks

  • Climate Basics: local projections, cyclone science, king tides, salt intrusion, heat stress.
  • Ocean Stewardship: coral gardening, crown-of-thorns control, mangrove nurseries, sustainable fisheries.
  • Energy & Water: solar microgrids, pico-hydro where feasible, rainwater harvesting, desalination basics.
  • Blue/Green Jobs: seaweed farming, sustainable aquaculture, eco-construction, GIS mapping, boat electrification.
  • Civic Literacy: rights, representation, consultation protocols, FPIC (free, prior, informed consent).

Delivery modes

  • School modules aligned to secondary and TVET; printable lesson packs for low-connectivity schools.
  • Community labs at churches and market houses; hands-on micro-grants for capstone projects.
  • Apprenticeships with local fishers, builders, rangers; elders as faculty for TEK (Traditional Ecological Knowledge).
  • Mobile kits (offline tablets, solar chargers); bilingual resources (Pijin/English) with audio versions.

Economic Pathways

Micro-enterprise • Sustainable fisheries • Eco-tourism • Cultural industries • Diaspora

Venture lanes (youth-friendly)

  • Seaweed & Sea Cucumber: climate-resilient mariculture with transparent co-ops and export standards.
  • Fish Processing Micro-hubs: solar ice, cold-chain carts, women-led value-add (smoking, packaging).
  • Eco-stays & Guides: reef-safe tourism bundles; diaspora referral programs; cyclone-season pivots.
  • Culture Studios: carving, music, dance, weaving; digital storefronts; IP & fair trade certifications.
  • Repair & Retrofit: solar repair, boat electrification, water filters, phone/mesh network upkeep.

Finance & safeguards

  • Micro-grants & ROSCAs: village-level rotating savings with youth/women seats.
  • Diaspora bridges: trust-based remittance matching; transparent dashboards.
  • Market charters: anti-harassment clauses, safe vendor routes, grievance desks.
  • Anti-extraction guardrails: contracts with FPIC, fair pricing floors, community benefit agreements.

Political Voice

Youth councils • Regional & UNFCCC representation • Safe advocacy

Structures

  • Youth Climate Councils: elected seats per ward; quotas for girls and persons with disabilities.
  • Delegation pipeline: training → briefs → representation at MSG/PIFS/UNFCCC.
  • Community consultation: village forums with FPIC-aligned minutes and public logs.

Safety & legitimacy

  • Nonviolence training; de-escalation; safe protest routes; legal aid cards.
  • Anti-tokenization protocol: youth co-signing on all press/partnerships.
  • Media brief kits with ocean-first narratives and data visuals; code of conduct.

Resilience & Survival

Food sovereignty • Mangroves & reefs • Climate-proof housing • Relocation frameworks

Nature-first defenses

  • Mangrove belts: nurseries, community planting calendars, legal protection zones.
  • Reef repair: coral gardening, rubble stabilization, heat-resilient strains, no-anchoring zones.
  • Watershed care: ridge-to-reef restoration; no-burn buffers; spring protection.

Homes, food, water

  • Storm-safe homes: raised floors, tie-downs, cross-bracing; local materials with cyclone standards.
  • Food sovereignty: breadfruit, cassava, swamp taro, agroforestry guilds; salt-tolerant varieties.
  • Water security: communal rain tanks, ceramic filters, emergency desal micro-kits.
Relocation & dignity

Last-resort community relocation respects graves, sacred sites, and fishing grounds. Framework includes FPIC, compensation, cultural mapping, and diaspora hosting plans.

Cultural Continuity

Language • Music • Rituals • Traditional Knowledge

Culture is infrastructure. Youth guardians record songs and stories (with consent), host inter-island exchanges, and design festivals aligned to planting and migration cycles. IP charters ensure benefits flow back to communities.

Tech & Media

Offline-first platforms • Secure comms • Storytelling • Gamified awareness

Design principles

  • Offline-first: content packs sync via SD cards/mesh; audio-first for low literacy.
  • Privacy: minimal data; opt-in only; trauma-aware moderation; no tracking in schools.
  • Open formats: printable PDFs, subtitles, radio scripts, teacher notes.

Features

  • Story studio: record elders; export to radio; caption in Pijin/English.
  • Quest engine: mangrove quests, water tests, roof-strap installs; badges unlocked offline.
  • Emergency mode: cyclone checklist, safe routes, first-aid flashcards; works fully offline.
