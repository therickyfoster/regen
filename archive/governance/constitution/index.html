üì¢ Enforcement Spectrum

Graduated responses ensuring proportional accountability

üí¨

Level 1: Gentle Guidance

Private feedback and advisory notes
‚ñº

Initial response to minor misalignments or potential concerns. Private communication offering constructive feedback, clarification of guidelines, and suggestions for better alignment.

Triggers: Unintentional misuse, unclear project descriptions, minor deviations from best practices.

üì¢

Level 2: Public Clarification

Distinguish aligned vs. independent use
‚ñº

Public statement clarifying the relationship between a project and Regen Archive values, distinguishing officially aligned work from independent projects using similar materials.

Purpose: Prevent confusion while allowing legitimate independent work to continue.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Level 3: Formal Misalignment Notice

Recorded warning with reputational impact
‚ñº

Official documentation of misalignment, published in the Ethics Log. Creates public record and reputational accountability while providing opportunity for correction.

Documentation includes: Nature of misalignment, evidence reviewed, recommendations for correction, timeline for response.

üö´

Level 4: Trademark Revocation

Loss of right to use Regen Archive marks
‚ñº

Removal of permission to use Regen Archive trademarks, names, and symbols. Project may continue using underlying open-source materials but cannot claim official alignment.

Applied when: Serious ethical violations, continued misalignment after formal notice, harm to regenerative mission.

üîÄ

Level 5: Protective Fork

Last resort safeguard against harmful use
‚ñº

Creation of stewarded fork to prevent misuse of critical infrastructure or knowledge. Reserved for cases where harmful use could cause significant damage to ecosystems or communities.

Process: Emergency review, community notification, technical implementation, transition planning.

üå± Bootstrapping Path

From pilot to global federation

Phase 1: Pilot Governance

Launch in a single city-node (e.g., RegenNode‚ÄìJakarta). Establish mini-circle with 3-4 trusted members spanning technical, ecological, and community expertise. Begin documenting decisions and building precedent.

Phase 2: Ethics Log Foundation

Publish first Ethics Log entries as precedent-setting examples. Develop standardized documentation processes. Create transparent decision-making protocols and community feedback mechanisms.

Phase 3: Regional Expansion

Scale to neighboring regions within the same bioregion. Establish coordination protocols between local circles. Begin quarterly bioregional coordination calls and shared project registries.

Phase 4: Global Federation

Connect regional circles into global governance network. Establish Global Circle with appeals processes. Mature the precedent archive into comprehensive decision-making resource for future stewards.
